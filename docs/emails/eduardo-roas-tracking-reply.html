1. Job ad application tracking

Yes — the conversion events we're sending to Meta cover the full funnel, including application-level actions on the specific job ads being advertised. The profile completion and survey events you saw are part of a broader set of signals we're passing back. This gives Meta's algorithm richer data to optimize delivery toward people who actually complete the process, not just those who click or start registration.

2. Financial data and ROI in monthly reports

Absolutely — and the timing on this is great. We've built a ROAS and CPA calculation engine that can generate financial performance metrics for every campaign, broken down by country. It calculates:

  • Target CPA — what we should be paying per acquisition (based on 20% of per-participant revenue)
  • Breakeven CPA — the maximum we can spend before a campaign becomes unprofitable
  • Actual CPA, ROAS, and ROI% — real performance against those targets
  • Recommended Budget — data-driven budget suggestions per country

To activate this for any campaign, we just need two pieces of information:

  1. Contract revenue size (total value of the contract)
  2. Total number of participants required

Once we have those two data points per campaign, we can start including full financial reporting — spend, CPA health (are we profitable, at target, or over?), ROAS, and ROI — in the monthly reports.
